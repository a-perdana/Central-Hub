Student AI Competency Framework

What students should be able to do with AI by the end of Primary, Lower Secondary, and Upper Secondary. Four areas of learning. Click any box to read the goals plus the classroom lessons that build them.

How to use this page

Students grow in AI literacy across four areas: Understanding AI, Using AI Responsibly, Evaluating AI Outputs, and AI and Society. The goals match the age group, not the calendar age.

The bottom row, "How to teach this", explains how to fit AI learning into your normal subject lessons. Click any box to open the goals and the lesson outlines that build them.

Strand 1
Understanding AI
Knows AI is a kind of technology that learns from examples. Names AI tools used at school and home. Knows AI is made by people, not magic.
Primary · S1
student.primary.strand1
Explains how generative AI works in simple terms. Knows what AI is good at and not good at. Knows AI can be wrong even when it sounds confident.
LS · S1
student.lower_secondary.strand1
Understands AI principles, types, and limits in more depth. Stays updated on AI. Sees how AI shapes future study and work in their field.
US · S1
student.upper_secondary.strand1
Strand 2
Using AI Responsibly
Uses approved AI tools with teacher help only. Asks adults for help. Doesn't share personal information with AI.
Primary · S2
student.primary.strand2
Uses approved AI tools for clear learning goals. Asks better questions to AI. Tells teachers when AI helped with work. Knows school rules.
LS · S2
student.lower_secondary.strand2
Uses AI well across many tasks. Picks tools that fit the task. Always discloses AI use. Uses AI for learning, not for taking shortcuts.
US · S2
student.upper_secondary.strand2
Strand 3
Evaluating AI Outputs
Knows AI can make mistakes. Checks AI answers with a teacher or a book. Doesn't believe AI just because it sounds sure.
Primary · S3
student.primary.strand3
Checks AI answers against trusted sources. Spots facts that look wrong. Sees when AI gives a one-sided view. Asks where the information comes from.
LS · S3
student.lower_secondary.strand3
Reviews AI deeply for facts, logic, and bias. Spots fake citations and Western-default framings. Defends own thinking with sources, not AI quotes.
US · S3
student.upper_secondary.strand3
Strand 4
AI and Society
Talks about where AI helps people (medicine, safety, access). Knows people have different views about AI. Discusses fairness in tech.
Primary · S4
student.primary.strand4
Discusses AI benefits and risks (jobs, privacy, fake info). Knows AI can be biased. Sees different sides. Knows their own role as a user.
LS · S4
student.lower_secondary.strand4
Discusses hard AI issues with nuance. Understands rules, accountability, transparency. Sees ethics frameworks. Prepares for AI-changed world.
US · S4
student.upper_secondary.strand4
How to teach this
Teacher guidance
Fits inside computing, science, English, PSHE. Direct AI use is teacher-led only. Students watch, not type.
Primary · Guide
student.primary.integration
Spread across subjects + clear computing lessons. Students use AI with a clear learning purpose and a teacher watching.
LS · Guide
student.lower_secondary.integration
Deeply built into all subjects + special tech courses. Big essays and projects include AI-use rules and show students' own thinking.
US · Guide
student.upper_secondary.integration
