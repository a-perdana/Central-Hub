AI Competency Framework v1.0

Institutional AI Maturity Framework

How well a school does with AI, looked at across five steps (Emerging to Leading) and six areas of school life. Schools rate themselves each year. The Eduversal team checks the rating during school visits.

Eduversal AI Competency Framework v1.0 · For Directors, Coordinators, Principals, Foundation Representatives

How to use this page

Five steps (Emerging → Leading) across six areas. Each box (5 × 6 = 30) says what the school looks like at that step in that area. Click a box to read the full description plus what to do next, what to never do, and which other pages to read.

Most Eduversal schools in 2026 are honestly at step 1 or 2 on most areas. That is normal. Marking a higher step than the school's real position hurts twice: the Eduversal team will see it, and the school will set goals it cannot reach. Pick two areas to grow by one step each year. Not all six.

Maturity Levels at a Glance

The Five Levels

1
Emerging
Beginning to engage. Awareness limited. No systematic approach.
2
Developing
Initiated with basic policies. Inconsistent implementation. Building capabilities.
3
Established
Coherent integration. Majority staff engagement. Consistent practices.
4
Advanced
Sophisticated integration. Strong impact. Contributes to network learning.
5
Leading
Outstanding. Network model. Contributes to broader practice.

Assessment process

Step 1
School self-assesses
Annual self-assessment at the start of the academic year. Involves leadership team, department heads, representative teachers. Honest baseline.
Step 2
Network appraisal validates
Eduversal Academic Directorate appraisal visit reviews evidence, observes practice, interviews staff and students, validates or adjusts ratings.
Step 3
Benchmarking and reporting
Network-level annual report. Schools see their position relative to peers. Priorities identified for the coming year.
