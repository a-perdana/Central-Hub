Sources & Further Reading

Where the reading content comes from

Every paragraph in this Learning Path is anchored to a published source. Skim this list when you have 20 minutes; bookmark anything you want to read in full. Sources are grouped by purpose — Cambridge first, Indonesian regulation second, evidence-based PD third, subject pedagogy fourth, AI policy fifth, Eduversal canon sixth. Click any group header to expand it.

6 groups · click to expand
  • Cambridge Teacher Standards (2023)
    The 5-domain, 27-standard reference every CTS chip on this page points back to. Read once end-to-end; refer to it whenever you are not sure why a competency anchors where it does.
    PDF · free · ~20 pages
  • Cambridge — Generative AI in Coursework (Exams Officers' Guide, Phase 3)
    The operating contract for AI use in coursework. Names what students CAN do (initial research + brief quotation with citation) and what counts as malpractice. Read before any AI-coursework conversation with a partner school.
    Web page · free · 10 min
  • Cambridge — Getting Started with AI in the Classroom (gswaic, 2024)
    Cambridge's 7-module teacher AI literacy framework. Use it as the structure when you author your subject's AI literacy CPD (competency xen-5).
    Cambridge Community resource · free · 2-3 hours
  • Cambridge — Hinge Questions (formative assessment resource)
    A concise Cambridge resource on Dylan Wiliam's hinge-question technique — short diagnostic checks at the moment the lesson "hinges". Anchor for csm-2 / cqa-3 formative-assessment work.
    PDF · free · 12 pages
  • Cambridge Professional Development Qualifications (PDQ)
    The path Specialists progress along externally. Levels 4 (Certificate) and 5 (Diploma) exist in Teaching with Digital Technologies, Bilingual Education, Educational Leadership. Coaching strand is the gap your Lead-level work fills.
    Cambridge page · free overview · qualification fees apply
  • Cambridge — The Cambridge approach to generative AI and assessment
    The position statement behind Cambridge's coursework AI rules. Reading this before the Exams Officers' Guide gives you the "why" behind the "what".
    Article · free · 8 min
  • Permendikdasmen No. 1 Tahun 2026 — Standar Proses
    The replacement for Permendikbudristek 16/2022 (effective 5 January 2026). Three new learning principles — sadar (conscious), bermakna (meaningful), menggembirakan (joyful). Anchors competencies nls-3 (curriculum policy) and cqa-1 (pacing — what "meaningful" pacing looks like).
    Indonesian official text · free · ~30 pages
  • Permendikdasmen No. 9 Tahun 2026 — Asesmen Nasional revision
    Replaces Permendikbudristek 17/2021 (effective 12 February 2026). Merges Tes Kemampuan Akademik (TKA) into Asesmen Nasional and introduces 8 dimensi profil pelajar. Anchor for cqa-3 (assessment analysis) — your EASE + Cambridge analysis sits next to this national stream.
    Indonesian official text · free · ~25 pages
  • SKB 7 Menteri — AI & Digital Technology in Education (12 March 2026)
    Joint decree by seven ministries on AI use in Indonesian education across all levels. Two principles: wise + smart digital-AI use and digital wellness. Anchor for xen-5 (Cambridge AI & Coursework) — Indonesia's national-policy layer above Cambridge's policy.
    Government press release · free · 10 min summary
  • UU No. 27 Tahun 2022 — Pelindungan Data Pribadi (PDP)
    Indonesia's GDPR-equivalent. Drives the data-handling discipline in nls-5 (Charter NN1+NN2) and any time you store student-facing video samples for observer calibration (cof-3 Advanced).
    Indonesian official text · free · 76 pages
  • Kurikulum Merdeka — Capaian Pembelajaran & Alur Tujuan Pembelajaran
    The national curriculum your partner schools deliver alongside Cambridge. Capaian Pembelajaran (CP) = learning outcomes by Fase; Alur Tujuan Pembelajaran (ATP) = scope & sequence. Anchor for cqa-2 (Scheme of Work moderation) — every Cambridge SoW in the network must align with KM at the same time.
    Government portal · free · varies by subject
  • EEF — Effective Professional Development Guidance Report (2024)
    Education Endowment Foundation's evidence synthesis. Names 14 mechanisms in 4 groups (build knowledge / motivate teachers / develop techniques / embed practice). Every workshop you design (tpd-1) should map to at least one mechanism from each group.
    PDF · free · 28 pages · the single most important PD reference on this page
  • EEF — A School's Guide to Implementation (2024)
    EEF's companion guidance on actually making change stick. 4 stages: Explore → Prepare → Deliver → Sustain. Sustain is where most CPD dies; tpd-4 Advanced is built around closing that gap.
    PDF · free · 36 pages
  • Kraft, Blazar & Hogan (2018) — The Effect of Teacher Coaching on Instruction and Achievement
    The 60-causal-study meta-analysis behind the claim "coaching produces +0.49 SD on instruction quality and +0.18 SD on student achievement". Cited in tpd-3 (1:1 coaching) and cof-4 (tracking coaching impact). If you read one paper on this list, read this one.
    Brown University working paper · free · 56 pages
  • Darling-Hammond, Hyler & Gardner (2017) — Effective Teacher Professional Development
    Learning Policy Institute review naming the 7 core features of effective PD: content focus, active learning, collaboration, modelling, coaching, feedback & reflection, sustained duration. Pre-EEF reference point.
    PDF · free · 76 pages
  • Desimone (2009) — Improving Impact Studies of Teachers' Professional Development
    The original "5 core features of effective PD" paper: content focus, active learning, coherence, duration, collective participation. Cited inside tpd-1 because workshop coherence is one of the most-skipped design moves.
    Journal article · paywalled (request via ResearchGate) · ~25 pages
  • Knight vs Bambrick-Santoyo — coaching model comparison
    Both are evidence-based; they suit different teachers. Knight Impact Cycles = facilitative (teacher names goal, picks action). Bambrick-Santoyo Get Better Faster = directive (Specialist names granular action steps). Mismatched style fails — tpd-3 Practitioner covers when to pick which.
    Blog article · free · 8 min
  • Rosenshine (2012) — Principles of Instruction
    10 evidence-based instructional principles distilled from cognitive science, classroom research, and master-teacher observation. Foundational across the Path — cited in csm-1, csm-2, tpd-2, and the EAL/ESL teaching moves throughout. Read in full; it is 12 pages.
    American Educator article · free · 12 pages · essential
  • Fletcher — Rosenshine's Principles in EAL Contexts (Part 1)
    An EAL teacher's translation of Rosenshine for second-language classrooms. Relevant for every ESL move in the Path: vocabulary pre-teach, scaffolded modelling, retrieval practice with command words.
    Blog article · free · 12 min
  • Karpicke & Roediger (2008) — The Critical Importance of Retrieval for Learning
    The retrieval-practice paper. Recalling-from-memory beats re-reading by a wide margin in long-term retention. Anchor for csm-2 (Command Word of the Day routine) and any daily-review move.
    Science journal article · free PDF · 4 pages
  • Wiliam — Designing Great Hinge Questions (ASCD)
    Dylan Wiliam's practical guide to formative assessment via hinge questions. Pairs with the Cambridge hinge-questions PDF above. Cited in csm-3 (Principal Examiner Reports → teaching intervention design).
    ASCD article · free · 10 min
  • City, Elmore, Fiarman & Teitel (2009) — Instructional Rounds in Education
    The discipline behind low-inference observation scripting (cof-1). Anchor for the move from "the lesson was engaging" to time-stamped quotes of what teacher and students actually said.
    Harvard Education Press book · paywalled · 304 pages
  • Catherine Lewis — Lesson Study research collection
    Japanese-origin practice now used worldwide. A group co-plans one lesson, one teacher delivers it while others observe with roles, the group debriefs, a different teacher delivers the revised version. Backbone of tpd-2 Advanced and tpd-5 (Subject CoP Facilitation).
    Research collection · free · ongoing
  • Sherrington — Rosenshine's Principles applied (Tom Sherrington / Third Space)
    A practitioner-friendly summary of Rosenshine principles grouped into 4 strands (sequencing, questioning, reviewing, stages of practice). Quicker entry point than the original paper if you need a hurry-up read.
    Blog article · free · 15 min
  • EEF Teaching and Learning Toolkit
    An ongoing meta-evidence map: every common classroom intervention rated by months of additional learning, evidence strength, and cost. Use it before recommending any intervention to a partner school.
    Web tool · free · search by intervention
  • UNESCO — AI Competency Framework for Teachers (2024)
    The international framework for teacher AI literacy. 5 aspects (Human-centred mindset, Ethics, AI foundations, AI pedagogy, AI for PD) × 3 progression levels (Acquire, Deepen, Create) = 15 blocks. Eduversal AICF anchors map back to this.
    PDF · free · 73 pages
  • Eduversal AICF — HQ Subject Specialist Playbook
    Your role's AI playbook inside Eduversal. Covers what AI may and may not do in curriculum authoring, item drafting, syllabus alignment work, and supporting teachers + schools. Read alongside Cambridge's coursework policy.
    Internal reference · free · search ?doc=aicf-* on /references
  • Eduversal AICF — Red Flags & Red Lines
    The network's documented AI red lines — what is malpractice, what is fine, what to escalate. Pair with Cambridge's Exams Officers' Guide Phase 3.
    Internal reference · free
  • Eduversal Academic Standards — Section 07: Teaching Quality & Practice
    The 27-madde core of teaching practice across the network. ES 7.3 (Appraisal System), 7.4 (Observation Process), 7.7 (Professional Learning Communities), 7.13–7.14 (AI in Teaching) appear repeatedly across this Path.
    Internal reference · free · navigate /references for all 23 sections
  • Eduversal Academic Standards — Section 17: Quality Assurance & Internal Review
    The QA backbone. ES 17.4 (Lesson Study + PLCs), 17.5 (Moderation), 17.6 (Benchmarking + Data Analysis), 17.13 (Continuous Improvement Culture) — almost every Advanced and Lead competency in this Path traces back here.
    Internal reference · free
  • Subject Specialist — First 90 Days Operational Guide
    Your operational ramp companion. 3 windows (Subject-Field Inheritance → Visit Cadence → Subject Steward), 40-week weekly checklist. The Path tells you the competencies; this handbook tells you the day-to-day rhythm.
    Internal handbook · free
  • Induction Charter — Non-Negotiables (NN1–NN5)
    The 5 network-wide non-negotiables. NN1 (induction data never feeds appraisal) and NN2 (HQ never reads named journal entries) sit at the core of nls-5 (Charter Boundary Discipline).
    Internal reference · free
  • Eduversal Teacher Appraisal Framework v2.1
    The network-wide teacher appraisal lens (F1–F5 + composite + A–F band). Every teacher appraisal you conduct on a partner-school visit lands here. Lives in `Academic Hub/resources/appraisal-framework-v2.json`, byte-identical copy in `Teachers Hub/resources/`.
    Internal reference · free
